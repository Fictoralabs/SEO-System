Classify the reason behind the search

Concept

EP04 starts keyword research, but not by collecting keywords first. Before collecting keywords, understand intent.

Keyword research is not only about high search volume and low keyword difficulty. In SEO and GEO, the important question is: what is the intent of the user searching this keyword?

There are four intent types shared in the reel: people searching to learn, people comparing options, people ready to buy, and people searching for specific brands. The intent decides whether the content should be a blog page, landing page, comparison page, or service page.

Task steps

  • Stop looking only at volume and difficulty. High search volume and low keyword difficulty keywords are not the starting point for SEO/GEO keyword research.
  • Ask the intent question. For every keyword, identify what the searcher is trying to do.
  • Classify the intent. Some searches are for learning, some are for comparing options, some are ready to buy, and some are for specific brands.
  • Map intent to page type. The intent decides whether the content should be a blog page, landing page, comparison page, or service page.

Intent types

Learning intent → blog or educational page.

Comparison intent → comparison or options page.

Buying intent → landing or service page.

Brand intent → brand or navigational page.
