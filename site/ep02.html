Concept document

Why page structure comes before writing

What is this process?

EP01 answered which pages the website may need. EP02 answers how a selected page should be structured before anyone writes the page.

The process starts with competitor pages selected from the EP01 matrix. Instead of guessing the outline, the sheet collects the headings already used by ranking or relevant competitors.

The heading scrape gives a clear content map: title tag, meta description, H1, H2, H3, and supporting sections. AI then compresses those patterns into a clean outline for the new page.

The goal is not to copy competitor wording. The goal is to understand the expected page structure and create a better, clearer outline for the website being built.

At a glance

Step 1 · Use the selected page type

Choose the page type from EP01, such as homepage, service page, location page, or comparison page. EP02 works best when one page type is reviewed at a time.

Step 2 · Collect competitor headings

Scrape the headings from selected competitor pages. Headings reveal what topics the page covers and in what order the information appears.

Step 3 · Generate the outline

Create a title tag, meta description, H1, and heading hierarchy. Keep it as a structure, not a finished article or landing-page copy.

Step 4 · Review before writing

Edit the generated outline for the actual brand, service, location, and offer. Then hand the structure to the writer or designer.

Limitations

What automation can and cannot do

Some websites block scraping

If a competitor blocks Apps Script requests, open the page manually and paste the visible headings into the EP02 PAGE INPUT row.

Headings may be messy

Some pages skip H1, use decorative headings, or repeat navigation text. Clean obvious noise before using AI output for a real page brief.

AI needs review

AI can find structure patterns, but it does not know the exact business, offer, compliance needs, or conversion goal unless you review and edit the final outline.

No copying

Use competitor headings as evidence, not as text to copy. The final structure should be original and better matched to the page being built.
