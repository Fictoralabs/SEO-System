Keyword Research Starts With Intent

EP03 & 04 is one keyword research workflow. First we define intent and page fit, then DataForSEO collects the keyword universe, and finally the SOP logic groups keywords into buckets, mapped pages, priorities, and outliers.

Intent layer
Page fit first

Data source
DataForSEO

Workflow

1 · Define intent rules

Intent mapping

2 · Add seed keywords

Human input

3 · Collect with DataForSEO

Use DataForSEO to pull keyword ideas, suggestions, related keywords, search volume, competition, difficulty, and intent data.

API collection

4 · Clean and deduplicate

Normalize casing, remove duplicates, merge repeated keyword rows, and keep the strongest metric row for each keyword.

Cleanup step

5 · Group into buckets

Group keywords that share the same intent and page fit. One bucket should usually become one page or one content asset.

SOP logic

6 · Prioritize the plan

Use volume, difficulty, intent value, business fit, conversion potential, and page relevance. Volume alone does not decide the strategy.

Decision output
Planned sheet views

The sheet should separate evidence from strategy

What EP03 & 04 will produce

The sheet should not dump thousands of raw keyword rows and call it strategy. Raw DataForSEO output is evidence. The useful output is the cleaned map: which keyword groups matter, what intent they represent, and which page should target them.

The final workflow should preserve the raw collection, but make the decision views simple enough for a business owner, SEO specialist, writer, or web designer to understand.

SOP logic

Use the old decision method with modern data collection

What stays

Collect broadly, group by same intent, name buckets by the page they should become, and keep outliers separate.

What changes

The old manual tool workflow becomes a DataForSEO workflow. The sheet should collect and enrich keywords automatically.

What gets better

The map can combine DataForSEO intent data, AI reasoning, and the actual page structure already planned in EP01 and EP02.

Intent classes

How keywords should be classified

Informational

The user wants to learn, understand, or solve a problem. Usually maps to guides, explainers, FAQ pages, and educational content.

Commercial

The user is comparing options, agencies, services, prices, or solutions. Usually maps to service pages, comparison pages, list pages, or buying guides.

Transactional

The user is close to taking action. Usually maps to landing pages, quote pages, booking pages, service pages, or conversion-focused location pages.

Navigational

The user is looking for a known brand, company, tool, person, or website. Usually becomes a brand note, competitor research input, or exclusion.
